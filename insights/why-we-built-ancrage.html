Why we built Ancrage

Sebastien Douville & Patrick Belliveau · 4 min read · March 2026

Ancrage did not start from a whiteboard.

For the past several years, our team has been building and deploying AI systems inside real organizations. A buy-side M&A advisory firm, a media company, a municipal government, a healthcare platform, logistics operators. We have seen what happens when AI lands well. We have seen, far more often, what happens when it does not.

The pattern is consistent. The technology is rarely the problem. The problem is everything that surrounds it: the truth map that nobody built, the workflow redesign that never happened, the change management that got skipped because it felt soft, the person who was supposed to own adoption who was never given the mandate or the time.

We built Gambit Technologies to prove that AI could deliver real outcomes. We built AskEllyn for breast cancer patients and watched it reach 15 countries in weeks. We built the outbound engine for Spearhead and watched it run thousands of qualified conversations without human involvement. We built the sales assistant for Bobit and watched it drive a 38% lead capture rate. Every one of those deployments taught us the same lesson.

The lesson

The technology, once you get it right, is the easy part.

The hard part is everything before it. Understanding the organization deeply enough to know where AI actually fits versus where it just appears to fit in a slide deck. And everything after it: keeping the system embedded in the actual workflows, the actual decisions, and the actual behavior of the people who have to live with it every day.

That is not a last-step problem. It is a full-organization problem. The organizations that genuinely thrive with AI don't deploy on top of themselves. They rewire themselves. From the data model up. From the process design through to the human behavior. With someone inside who can orchestrate all of it at once and stay until it's real.

What we decided to build

Ancrage is the answer to that lesson.

We are not a software company. We are not a consultancy. We are an embedded operating partner. A team that goes inside your organization, builds the truth map of how it actually works, redesigns and deploys AI systems against that truth, and manages the human side of the rewiring with the discipline it takes to make change permanent.

Our economics are tied to outcomes. We work with a small number of clients at any given time. We are selective, because our model only works if your organization genuinely improves.

We operate in the verticals where operational complexity is highest: manufacturing, logistics and supply chain, healthcare, defense, energy, mining, and financial services. Many of our clients are PE-backed and moving fast. These are environments where the difference between a transformation that sticks and one that stalls is measured in EBITDA, not slide decks.

Why now

Anthropic and OpenAI are both building forward-deployed consulting arms. The two leading AI labs have independently concluded that enterprise AI is bottlenecked on implementation. They will solve it at scale, for the largest organizations. The mid-market is a different problem, and it will remain underserved by generalist teams without deep vertical knowledge.

We have been solving it for years. We built Ancrage to do it at scale.

If you are running a complex organization and trying to close the gap between what AI can do and what your operation actually does, that is the work we are here for.

We're glad you found us.
