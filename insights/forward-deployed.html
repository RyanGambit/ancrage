What forward-deployed actually means

Patrick Belliveau · 4 min read · March 2026

The phrase "forward-deployed" has started appearing in a lot of conversations about enterprise AI. Palantir made it famous. Anthropic and OpenAI are now using variations of it to describe their new PE-backed consulting arms. It has become, in some circles, a synonym for "expensive consultant who shows up in person."

That is not what it means. Or at least, it is not what it should mean.

Forward-deployed is a military term. It describes a unit that operates from inside the territory. Not from headquarters far behind the front, but inside the environment where the actual work is happening. The forward-deployed unit does not advise from a distance. It operates from within.

Advisory produces recommendations. Embedded produces outcomes.

The difference is accountability. An advisor can tell you what to do and leave before you find out if it worked. An embedded operator is still there when the system hits the real organization. When the process maps turn out to be fiction, when the team that was supposed to use the tool decides they preferred the old way, when the data that was supposed to be clean turns out not to be.

The embedded operator has to solve those problems because they are standing in the middle of them. That changes everything: who does the work, how long they stay, what they are optimized for, and how they get paid.

Why the economics have to reflect it

Most consulting engagements are priced on time and materials. You pay for the hours, and the outcome is your problem. That model works when the deliverable is a document. It works poorly when the deliverable is an organizational rewiring that has to stick.

When we embed with a client, our economics are tied to outcomes. If we build a system that nobody uses, we should not get paid like we built something valuable. If we drive a measurable improvement in operational performance, we should participate in the value we created. This is not a marketing position. It is a structural requirement of doing the work honestly.

It also means we are selective. We do not embed with organizations where we do not believe we can drive measurable outcomes. Our business only works if our clients' businesses genuinely improve.

What it actually takes

Being genuinely forward-deployed requires people who can operate inside the real organization, not just the official one. It requires technical depth and domain knowledge together. A great AI engineer who does not understand logistics operations will build technically perfect systems the logistics team routes around. A great logistics operator who does not understand AI will make accurate diagnoses and wrong implementation choices.

It also requires staying longer than most engagements stay. The hardest part of any transformation is not the design. It is the six months after the design, when the organization has to actually change, when the people who preferred the old way are still there, when the edge cases emerge that nobody anticipated.

That is the work. That is what forward-deployed means. And that is why Ancrage was built to do it.
