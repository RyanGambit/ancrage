Why the mid-market needs a different answer

Sebastien Douville · 5 min read · March 2026

Something remarkable happened in early 2026. Anthropic entered talks with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Permira. OpenAI began conversations with Advent, Bain Capital, Brookfield, and TPG. Both want the same thing: forward-deployed engineers selling AI implementation directly into PE portfolio companies.

When the two leading AI labs independently conclude they need humans in the work to distribute their technology, the message is clear: enterprise AI is bottlenecked on implementation. On the gap between what the technology can do and what organizations are actually able to do with it.

But the reason for that gap is more fundamental than most people acknowledge. And the solution is more demanding than deploying a few forward-deployed engineers.

The real reason AI deployments stall

Most organizations that struggle with AI deployment are not struggling because the technology failed. They are struggling because the deployment was built on top of the organization's official version of itself. The process maps, the org charts, the stated workflows. Rather than the real one.

Every organization has a gap between how it says it works and how it actually works. The official version lives in documentation. The real version lives in the heads of the people who have been there long enough to know which data is trustworthy, which approval is ceremonial, which system of record is actually used. Most AI systems are trained on the official version. They hit the real organization and stall.

This is not a last-step problem. It is a foundation problem. You cannot patch the final stage of a deployment when the foundation was never built correctly. The fix is not better tooling at the margin. It is a genuine rewiring of the organization, from the data model up.

What a rewiring actually requires

Organizations that genuinely thrive with AI share a common pattern. They do not deploy AI on top of existing processes. They redesign the processes, rebuild the underlying data architecture, and manage the human change. All simultaneously, from the inside.

This requires three things that most vendors and most consulting firms do not provide together. First: deep organizational mapping. Building the truth map of how the organization actually works, not how it says it works. Second: strategic orchestration. Redesigning workflows and deploying AI systems against that truth, not on top of the fiction. Third: people-first change management. The sustained, embedded work of changing behavior across the organization until the new way of working is the only way anyone remembers.

None of these can be sequenced. They have to happen together. And they have to happen from inside the organization, not from a distance.

Why the mid-market is underserved

The Anthropic and OpenAI JVs will reach the largest and most sophisticated portfolio companies. They will be generalist by design. Their portfolios are too diverse to go deep on any single industry or function. A healthcare operator and a logistics network and a defense contractor have almost nothing in common from a deployment standpoint.

The mid-market has different needs. It requires operators who have lived inside the function, who understand the actual workflows in the actual verticals, and who can stay long enough to see the rewiring through.

The AI labs just told you where the gaps will be. We have been building for this moment for years.
